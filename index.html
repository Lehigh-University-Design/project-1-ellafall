The History of the Internet: net.art

‘Internet Art’ or ‘Net.art’: a form of art specifically conceived through the internet, on the internet, and for the internet.

1980s: Emergence of Digital Art

Artists begin experimenting with digital technologies, exploring the creative potential of computers and networks.

1990: Birth of Net.art

The term "net.art" is coined by artists Vuk Cosic and Josephine Bosma.

Early net.artists focus on using the internet as a medium for artistic expression, often incorporating interactivity and collaboration.

Mid-1990s: Pioneering Works

Visit the art piece here!

Artists such as Jodi (Joan Heemskerk and Dirk Paesmans) and Olia Lialina create pioneering net.art works, challenging traditional notions of art and audience participation.

Mid-1996: Documenta X

Net.art gains international recognition at Documenta X, an influential contemporary art exhibition in Kassel, Germany, with the inclusion of net.art projects.
